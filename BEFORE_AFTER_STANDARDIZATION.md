### Query 3: "What is diabetes?"
```markdown
## üìã Executive Summary

Diabetes is a chronic metabolic disorder characterized by elevated blood sugar levels due 
to insufficient insulin production (Type 1) or insulin resistance (Type 2), requiring 
medical management through medication, lifestyle changes, and monitoring.

---

## üîç Detailed Analysis

Diabetes mellitus is a metabolic disorder characterized by persistently high blood glucose 
(sugar) levels. The condition affects how your body processes glucose, the primary source 
of energy for cells.

**Types of Diabetes:**

**Type 1 Diabetes:**
- Autoimmune condition where the immune system attacks insulin-producing cells
- Usually diagnosed in children and young adults
- Accounts for 5-10% of diabetes cases
- Requires insulin therapy for life
- Not preventable with current knowledge

**Type 2 Diabetes:**
- Most common form (90-95% of cases)
- Body becomes resistant to insulin or doesn't produce enough
- Often develops in adults over 45, but increasingly in younger people
- Related to obesity, physical inactivity, genetics
- May be prevented or delayed through lifestyle changes
- Initially managed with diet, exercise, oral medications
- May eventually require insulin

**Gestational Diabetes:**
- Develops during pregnancy
- Usually resolves after childbirth
- Increases risk of Type 2 diabetes later

**Common Symptoms:**
- Increased thirst and frequent urination
- Extreme hunger
- Unexplained weight loss (Type 1)
- Fatigue and irritability
- Blurred vision
- Slow-healing sores
- Frequent infections

**Complications if Uncontrolled:**
- Cardiovascular disease
- Nerve damage (neuropathy)
- Kidney damage (nephropathy)
- Eye damage (retinopathy, potential blindness)
- Foot damage (poor circulation, infections)
- Skin and mouth conditions

**Treatment Approaches:**
- Blood glucose monitoring
- Insulin therapy (Type 1, sometimes Type 2)
- Oral medications (Type 2)
- Healthy diet (controlled carbohydrates)
- Regular physical exercise
- Weight management
- Blood pressure and cholesterol control

Early diagnosis and proper management are crucial for preventing complications and 
maintaining quality of life.
```
